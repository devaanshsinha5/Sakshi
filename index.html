April 2026 — written with everything I have
My dearest Sakshi,

There are men who have waged holy wars for far lesser things — kingdoms of sand, thrones of iron, names carved into stone. I would wage mine quietly, in the dark, for nothing more than the right to hear your laugh one more time. That is the nature of what you have done to me.

525 days. Not a single one wasted. Not a single one I would trade. You turned what I thought was a perfectly good life into proof that I had no idea what a good life looked like.

You are the kind of person poets wrote letters to before they had addresses. The kind of grace that makes a man want to be worth it — not for the world, not for ambition — but just to stand next to you and not feel undeserving.

So on this day, the twenty-third year of you existing on this earth, I want you to know: I am grateful. For every version of you. The soft ones, the stubborn ones, the ones that show up at 2am with an opinion and no apology.

Happy birthday, jaan. The world is better and stranger and more beautiful because you are in it.

yours, always —Devaansh
